Why GAP QPA will not work?
Why scalar‑weighted relations are essential for perverse sheaf models. The scalar coefficients in our quadratic relations encode critical geometric data, such as monodromy weights, intersection numbers, or the multiplicities of edge flows between brain regions. Removing these scalars – i.e., reducing the algebra to a radical‑square‑zero quotient – collapses all such distinctions, forcing every length‑2 path to zero and thereby destroying the structure needed to represent perverse sheaves or their deformation theory. In particular, the resulting algebra would no longer capture the relative strengths of connections or the gluing data along strata, making it unsuitable for any meaningful sheaf‑theoretic interpretation. Therefore, preserving the scalar‑weighted inhomogeneous relations is not optional for our application.

Limitations of GAP/QPA for inhomogeneous path algebra relations. While the QPA package within GAP (accessed via SageMath) provides powerful tools for computing homological invariants of finite‑dimensional quotients of path algebras, it is fundamentally restricted to admissible ideals generated by homogeneous relations – i.e., all terms in each relation must have the same path length. Our algebra includes scalar‑weighted relations of the form length‑2 path = scalar × length‑1 arrow, which are inhomogeneous. Such relations are not admissible (they do not lie in the square of the radical), and they cause QPA’s Gröbner basis and homological algorithms to fail, producing errors such as “Not a bound quiver algebra” and “no method found for Coefficients”. Moreover, QPA does not natively support computing Hochschild cohomology for inhomogeneous quotients, and even basic operations like dimension and Cartan matrix become inaccessible. Consequently, despite the convenience of the Sage‑GAP interface, QPA is unsuitable for our model. We therefore turned to Macaulay2 with its PathAlgebras package, which handles inhomogeneous relations naturally and provides the necessary homological algebra.
